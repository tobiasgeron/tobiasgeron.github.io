Teaching Overview

I have taught an undergraduate course titled "Introduction to Stars and Galaxies" twice: first as co-instructor in the Summer of 2024, and then as the sole head instructor in the Summer of 2025. This course, aimed at non-science majors, had 196 students enrolled in the most recent iteration. I found it deeply rewarding to share my enthusiasm for astronomy with students and am committed to supporting an engaging and supportive learning environment. Topics that we discussed include, but are not limited to: special relativity, general relativity, the twin paradox, black holes, neutron stars, the Sun, the life cycle of stars, the Milky Way, galaxy morphology, dark matter, Hubble's Law, the expansion of the Universe, dark energy, and the Big Bang. This course also included a few telescope nights, see picture on the left below. My overall score on the course evaluations was notably higher than the divisional average (4.7/5 vs 4.2/5), and I scored equal to or above the divisional average on all criteria. The figure on the right shows the results from the evaluations for a few selected criteria. Full course evaluations are available upon request.
